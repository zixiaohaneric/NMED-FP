The Rise of Chinese Animation in 2010
The Development History of Domestic Anime
Havoc in heaven
The early exploration stage of Chinese animation can be traced back to the early 20th century, when the animation industry was almost blank in China. However, with their love and exploratory spirit for animation art, their predecessors embarked on a difficult path of exploration.
In terms of technology, early Chinese animation mainly relied on traditional hand drawn techniques. Artists need to draw frames by frames, and the birth of each animated work requires a lot of manpower and time. For example, in 1926, the Wan brothers produced China's first animated film, "Da Nao Studio," which was completed by hand under rudimentary conditions. Subsequently, over the course of several decades, hand drawn techniques have continuously developed and improved, from simple line outlines to gradually enriched color applications, accumulating valuable production experience for Chinese animation.
The creative experience, technical accumulation, and audience base accumulated by these early classic anime works provided strong support for the explosive development of the Chinese anime industry around 2010, becoming an indispensable and important stage in the development of Chinese anime.
The outbreak period around 2010
Around 2010, China's domestic animation industry experienced explosive growth, driven by various factors to reach new heights.
In terms of production technology, traditional hand drawing is gradually transitioning towards digitalization. Animation production companies have begun to introduce advanced digital drawing software, 3D modeling, and animation production tools, which have greatly improved production efficiency and image quality. For example, in the past, drawing a complex frame of image may have required the artist to spend hours or even days. After digitization, with the convenience of drawing software, the drawing time has been greatly reduced, and the lines of the image are smoother and the colors are more accurate. In the production of "Nano Core", 3D modeling technology is extensively used to create realistic sci-fi scenes and character images. The mecha settings are finely crafted to every texture, giving the audience a strong visual impact and marking a new level of visual presentation in domestic animation.
The Influence of Japanese Anime in China
Detective Conan
In terms of cultural dissemination, it cleverly showcases Japan's urban style and traditional customs, promoting cultural exchanges between China and Japan. The image of Conan has deeply penetrated people's hearts, giving birth to numerous fan communities in China, with lively online and offline communication, promoting the spread of detective culture among young people, and inspiring the creation of some detective themed anime in China.
NARUTO
Since its serialization in 2002, it has gained a massive fan base in China through the fusion of passionate battles and deep emotions. The plot revolves around the ninja boy Naruto, who carries the dream of becoming Naruto and goes through countless difficult challenges to establish a life and death bond with his companions.
Attack on Titan
It caused a sensation in China's anime circle around 2010. The story is set in a world where humans are threatened by giants and rise up in rebellion for survival. Its unique doomsday worldview, tense and exciting plot, and exploration of profound themes such as human nature and freedom have broken the narrative framework of traditional anime, bringing strong shock to Chinese audiences.
