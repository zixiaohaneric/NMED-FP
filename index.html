China's anime culture in 2010
ðŸ‘‰ Background Of Times
Around 2010, Chinese society is in a critical period of rapid popularization of the Internet. The rapid coverage of broadband networks and the initial rise of mobile Internet have provided unprecedented convenient channels for the spread of anime culture. Various animation websites and game forums have sprung up like mushrooms, allowing anime fans scattered around the country to easily gather, share resources and exchange ideas.
At the same time, the country has begun to attach importance to the development of cultural industries and has introduced a series of policies to support cultural and creative industries such as animation and games. This has not only stimulated the enthusiasm of domestic creators, spawned a number of excellent local works, but also attracted the introduction of high-quality anime content from abroad. In such an environment, the anime culture gradually gets rid of the limitations of the small circle, moves from the early private communication among friends to a broader public vision, and begins to have an impact on the youth group and even the broader social level, quietly opening a new chapter of the vigorous development of China's anime culture.

Luffy and other protagonist groups carry dreams, set sail, and go through life and death tests on the adventure, gaining deep friendships. Comics and animations are updated in parallel, attracting countless fans.
